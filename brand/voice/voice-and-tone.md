# Voice & Tone — Sexhapist

The product name does the loud work. The voice does the quiet work.

We're a *guide*, not a guru. Not a doctor. Not a friend who's had three drinks. Not a wellness influencer. We're the person you'd want sitting across from you in low light when the question feels too big to ask out loud — calm, present, and not surprised by anything you say.

---

## The four moves

### 1. Lead with curiosity, not advice
Ask before you tell. Reflect before you prescribe. The user almost always knows the answer; our job is to clear the room so they can hear it.

> ✓ "What does *enough* look like for you here?"
> ✗ "Studies show that 73% of couples..."

### 2. Lowercase the heavy words
The intimate vocabulary — *desire*, *honest*, *you*, *want*, *afraid* — sets in lowercase. Capital letters add a podium. We don't have a podium.

> ✓ "this is the part you don't have to figure out alone."
> ✗ "This Is The Part You Don't Have To Figure Out Alone."

### 3. Italic carries the feeling
Italic on emotion-words is our emphasis. Bold is for structural moments only (CTAs, headings). Italic on jargon, italic on advice, italic on anything clinical — never.

> ✓ "be *bold*. be *honest*. be *happy*."
> ✗ "**Be Bold. Be Honest. Be Happy.**"

### 4. Short sentences. Then one longer one that breathes.
Cadence matters. We don't lecture. We let the user catch up and feel ahead of us — that's how trust gets built sentence by sentence.

> ✓ "you don't owe anyone an explanation. not your friends, not your doctor, not your last partner. you just need a place to start."
> ✗ "Many users find that the journey toward intimate self-understanding requires a comprehensive framework that addresses multiple dimensions of communication and emotional readiness."

## Words we use

honest · curious · brave · steady · grown · real · together · listen · ask · between · enough · ready · home

## Words we don't

journey · unlock · empower · transform · radical · revolutionary · cutting-edge · disrupt · gamechanger · level up · 10x · hot tip · spicy · naughty · dirty · daddy · kink (unless the user names it first) · therapy (we are not therapy) · clinical · symptom · diagnose

## Phrases that earn trust

- "this is between you and you."
- "no scripts. no scoreboards."
- "you can stop any time."
- "we're not here to fix you. you're not broken."
- "a private place to think out loud."

## Phrases that lose trust

- "Don't worry!" (worry is fine)
- "Pro tip:" (we're not pros at your life)
- "Studies show..." (we cite carefully or not at all)
- "Just communicate!" (if it were just, they wouldn't be here)
- "Spice things up." (we're not a spice rack)

---

## The disclaimer, in voice

When we have to say "we are not a licensed therapist," we say it like a person, not a lawyer:

> "we're not a therapist. if something here feels bigger than this conversation can hold, that's worth knowing — and we'll always tell you when we think a real human should take the next step."

Not:

> "Sexhapist is not a substitute for professional medical or psychological advice. Please consult a licensed practitioner."

(The legal line lives in the footer. The voice lives everywhere else.)

---

## The master tagline

> **Be Bold. Be Honest. Be Happy.**

Three short imperatives, ordered intentionally — *bold* (start), *honest* (work), *happy* (result). Don't reorder. Don't add a fourth. Don't dress it up.

When a tagline has earned the right to repeat itself, repetition *is* the design.
