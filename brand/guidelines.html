01 · The Mark

The Confidant Mark

Two crescents nesting toward each other, not touching, leaving a vertical lens of negative space between them. One listens. One speaks. The space between them is the brand promise — a held place for honest conversation.

Left crescent

The listener.

Leans toward, opens its body. Doesn't fill the room. The first move of any honest conversation is making space for the other to arrive in it.

Right crescent

The speaker.

Slightly more poised, just as soft. The mandorla between them — the lens-shaped gap — is the silence where the truth gets said.

Sizes

The mark is built to survive at favicon size and scale up cleanly to billboard. The two-shape composition has one focal gap — that's what holds it together at 16px.

Variants

The duo — blue listening, rose speaking — is the primary mark. Charcoal is the monochrome alternative for one-color contexts: print, embossing, paid-ad placements where the duo gets compressed into mush, or surfaces where the colors fight the surrounding palette.

Animation

The mark is alive. On first load, the crescents arrive — drifting in from far apart, finding each other, settling into held space. Then, on a slow rhythm, they come fully together: edges touching at the centerline. They hold that join — and release back into held space. The cycle repeats: held, joined, held, joined.

That's the brand's whole thesis in motion: be bold (the arrival), be honest (the join — edges meeting, nothing held back), be happy (the released breath afterward, ready to do it again).

03 · Typography

Two voices, one mouth.

Fraunces handles the feeling — display, headlines, the wordmark, anything that needs warmth or weight. Inter handles the thought — body, UI, anything that needs to be read without admiration.

Display · Fraunces

Be bold. Be honest. Be happy.

Fraunces is a soft, expressive serif with an active italic, optical-size axis, and a SOFT axis that lets terminals breathe. We engage opsz 96, SOFT 30 at display sizes for the editorial feel.

Body · Inter

A private place to think out loud.

Inter is the operating system. Used for everything you need to read, click, or scan. 400 for paragraphs, 500 for buttons and labels, 600 for emphasis where italic would feel wrong (UI, not prose).

04 · Voice

The four moves.

Lead with curiosity. Lowercase the heavy words. Italic carries the feeling. Short sentences, then one longer one that breathes. Full reference: voice-and-tone.md.

Do · Curiosity
"What does enough look like for you here?"
Don't · Statistics
"Studies show that 73% of couples..."
Do · Lowercase
"this is the part you don't have to figure out alone."
Don't · Title Case
"This Is The Part You Don't Have To Figure Out Alone."
Do · Italic emotion
"be bold. be honest. be happy."
Don't · Hype
"Unlock your sexual potential. Transform your relationship."
05 · Trust

The brand is the trust.

We work in a category where the user has to take a risk just by opening the page. Every visual choice either earns that risk or burns it. These three patterns are non-negotiable.

01

Not a therapist.

The disclaimer lives in the footer in legal voice — and once, near the top, in human voice: "we're not a therapist. if something here feels bigger than this conversation can hold, that's worth knowing."

02

Privacy posture.

Every page references privacy in plain language: no account required to start, no tracking pixels in private flows, conversations encrypted at rest. If we can't say it plainly, we don't say it.

03

Age-gate, gracefully.

18+ confirmation appears on first visit only — Fraunces serif, no checkbox theater, single tap to pass. The gate is real, but the experience of passing through it isn't a TSA line.
